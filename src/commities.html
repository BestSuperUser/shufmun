OUR COMMITIES
1. DISARMAMENT AND INTERNATIONAL SECURITY COMMITEE (DISEC)
The First Committee of the United Nations General Assembly is one of the six main committees in the United Nations General Assembly. It concerns about disarmament and international security issues. The First Committee holds a 4-5 week meeting in October each year after the General Assembly, General Debate. Agenda item: "Preventing the use of weapons of mass destruction" will be discussed.
2. UNITED NATIONS OFFICE ON DRUGS AND CRIME (UNODC):
The United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime was established as a result of the merger of the United Nations International Drug Control Program and the Crime Prevention and Criminal Justice Divisions at the United Nations Office in Vienna in 1997. Agenda item: "International cooperation in the fight against drug trafficking’’ will be discussed.
3. SOCIAL, HUMANITARIAN AND CULTURAL COMMITTEE (SOCHUM)
The third committee of the general assembly, known as the Humanitarian and Cultural Committee, is a United Nations body that focuses on a wide range of issues related to the protection of human rights and fundamental freedoms. This committee which is mainly guided by the Universal Declaration of Human Rights has a wide field of action and deals with a variety of social and cultural issues.It cooperates with various organizations such as UNESCO, FAO and UNISEF to work on the implementation of committee decisions. Agenda item: "protection of workers' rights in developing countries" will be discussed.
4. UNITED NATIONS ENVIRONMENT PROGRAMME (UNEP)
UNEP aims to coordinate the environmental issue at the United Nations, to constantly review the state of the environment at the global level, to attract the attention of the international community on environmental issues, and to ensure the development of international and national environmental policy and law. Agenda item : "Long-term measures that can be taken to combat global climate change" will be discussed.
5. UNITED NATIONS INTERNATIONAL CHILDREN'S EMRGENCY FUND (UNICEF)
It was established in 1946 as the United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund, and later in 1954, it was mandated by the United Nations General Assembly to carry out promotional and advocacy activities for the protection of children's rights, to help meet the basic needs of children and to create opportunities for children to realize their full potential. Although the words "international" and "urgent" have been deleted, the abbreviation has not changed. Agenda item: "Measures that can be taken to prevent child labor" will be discussed.
